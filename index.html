Ontological Fidelity Scorecard

7 structural questions every Clinical AI team should answer before deployment
Most Clinical AI systems fail not because of bad algorithms, but because they train on structurally distorted data. Billing codes mistaken for diagnoses. Administrative categories treated as clinical reality. This scorecard identifies the 7 most common architectural blind spots.
0-3 Yes High Risk
4-5 Yes Partial Coverage

# Diagnostic Question Design Pattern Yes No Partial
1 Does your system annotate coding fidelity at data ingestion?A patient coded J06.9 for billing may present with early pneumonia. Does your pipeline know the difference? Coding-Fidelity Annotation
2 Do you preserve low-frequency clinical signals in your training data?Rare but critical findings (early-stage malignancy, atypical presentations) get drowned out by high-volume routine codes. Signal Preservation
3 Do you monitor semantic drift, not just statistical drift?When ICD-10 redefines a code or billing guidelines shift its usage, your model's learned associations become silently wrong. Semantic Drift Detection
4 Do you maintain parallel clinical and administrative representations?"Chest pain, unclear origin" and billing code "I20.9 Angina pectoris" describe different things. Your system needs both layers. Dual Representation
5 Do you track the AI influence ratio in your training data?If AI-suggested codes feed back into training data, the model reinforces its own biases. This reification loop is invisible without tracking. Feedback Loop Monitoring
6 Do you treat terminology updates as schema migrations?ICD, SNOMED, and LOINC update annually. Without migration logic, your model trains on a shifting vocabulary it cannot detect. Terminology Governance
7 Do you have a pluggable EU AI Act / MDR compliance layer?Hard-coded compliance breaks every time regulations update. A modular layer lets you swap rules without retraining. Regulatory Modularity
